# Huntertown Gardens — concept website (spec build #3)

Concept/spec website for **Huntertown Gardens**, 14 County Road 70, Laotto,
IN 46763 · (260) 637-8585. "The original Farmer's Market since 1937." Built
by It's Already Done Web Co. from public information — **owners have not seen
or approved it**; footer carries the concept disclaimer. Portfolio piece on
alreadydoneweb.com.

## Facts used (researched July 2026)

- Founded 1937: Joella Decker set a bushel of potatoes with a for-sale sign
  by the road in Huntertown; grew to a 4-acre truck farm with husband Charles
- Joella died 1977; son Tom & wife Sharon took over 1983 and moved the market
  to LaOtto (kept the Huntertown name); today run by Sharon Decker (CEO) and
  daughters Theresa Shultz, Rose Hathaway, Elizabeth Decker — 5th generation
  of women; survived two fires and a tornado; sends clothes/seeds to a
  partner village in Ukraine
- 6,000 sq ft market; hours Mon–Sat 9–6, Sun 11–6; SEASONAL ~late March to
  ~Thanksgiving (varies year to year — site says "check Facebook")
- Spring: bedding plants, vegetable starts, hanging baskets (Memorial Day
  sale tradition), seeds. Summer: sweet corn, tomatoes/canning tomatoes,
  melons, Red Haven peaches, Stanley plums, green beans — five produce
  deliveries weekly, some supplier relationships 45+ years. Fall: mums,
  pumpkins, gourds, corn stalks, Indian corn, Honey Crisp apples
- Farm store: Amish cheese, fresh butter, bacon, bulk old-fashioned candy,
  jams, spices, canned goods, cookies & cinnamon rolls, Plum Creek soft
  pretzels. Major cards accepted; free parking; wheelchair accessible
- 4.6★ (216 Google reviews). huntertowngardens.com is PARKED FOR SALE on
  Afternic — key pitch fact. Do NOT name Thomas Decker as current owner
  (BBB stale; he appears to have died in 2020)

## Design

Heritage farm market: cream/kraft paper bg, botanical deep green primary,
terracotta accent, harvest gold. Fonts: Young Serif (display) + Karla (body).
Motif: "EST. 1937" roundel, seed-packet style cards, timeline on story page.
4 pages: index / seasons (What's in Season) / story / visit.
Hero uses hero-market-crop2.png (original had a wrong AI-generated sign;
cropped out — Gemini credits were exhausted so edit-by-reference wasn't
available; top up at ai.studio to regenerate).
